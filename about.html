About me

Designer who believes good work should do good

I'm Jonathan Bowden, a digital designer based in Melbourne. I've spent the past decade working at the intersection of product strategy, user experience, and visual design—helping teams build things that are useful, usable, and quietly considered.

My focus is on digital design for good: projects that have a positive impact on people's lives, whether that's tools for education, climate, health, or public services. I believe thoughtful design can make complex systems feel simple and help people do their best work.

I'm drawn to early-stage problems where the shape of the solution isn't obvious yet. I like working closely with founders, engineers, and subject-matter experts to figure out what to build—then making it feel inevitable.

What I value

Clarity over cleverness

Good design explains itself. I aim for interfaces that feel obvious.

Ship small, learn fast

The smallest honest version teaches more than a perfect spec.

Kindness in details

Microinteractions, error states, and edge cases deserve care.

Words matter

Clear language is a design decision. I write as part of designing.
